Frequently Asked Questions

1. Do you jump-start cars using another car battery?

No. We do not rely on another vehicle’s battery for jump-starts. Our technicians use professional-grade jump starters designed specifically for roadside assistance. These tools provide controlled power, reduce the risk of voltage spikes, and protect your vehicle’s electrical system. This approach is safer for modern vehicles and eliminates the risk of damage that can come from traditional car-to-car jump-starting.

2. Do I need to be present when I call for a jump-start?

Yes, in most cases, we require the vehicle owner or an authorized representative to be present at the time of service. This allows us to confirm vehicle access, ensure safety, and get approval before performing the jump-start or any additional service. If you’re unable to be present, let us know in advance; alternative arrangements may be possible depending on the situation and vehicle access.

3. What happens if I need an extra cleaning service before my scheduled day?

We understand that sometimes usage is higher than anticipated. If you find your unit needs an additional service, just give our office a call. We can dispatch a truck for an unscheduled service call for an additional fee.

4. Can I call you for battery installation only if I already have a battery?

Yes. If you already have a compatible battery, our technician can come to your location and handle the installation for you. We’ll confirm the battery fits your vehicle, install it correctly, and test the system to make sure everything is working as it should before we leave.

5. Do you jump-start cars using another car battery?

No. We do not rely on another vehicle’s battery for jump-starts. Our technicians use professional-grade jump starters designed specifically for roadside assistance. These tools provide controlled power, reduce the risk of voltage spikes, and protect your vehicle’s electrical system. This approach is safer for modern vehicles and eliminates the risk of damage that can come from traditional car-to-car jump-starting.
